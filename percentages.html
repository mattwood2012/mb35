Moving Beyond 3.5
Playing the Percentages

Most pickleball games are lost not won. Watch some 3.5 level games and compare how often points are won with a great shot versus how often points are lost by an unforced error.

One of the key takeaways from this series of clinics is for you to play in a way that will significantly reduce your unforced errors while at the same time increasing your opponents unforced errors.

To do this we need to consistently hit shots within our capabilities and our opponents will take care of winning for us.

If you win 55% (or more) of points, when you serve and when you receive, you will win every game you play!

On the other hand, if you play a shot that you can make 80% of the time you only have a 51% likelihood of making it 3 times in a row and that drops to 41% for 4 in a row. If you can make the shot 90% of the time your 3/4 shot success rate jumps to 73%/ 62%

So, a key strategy for success is to have a few shots that you play consistently well. For example, a simple forehand “tip pointing down” shot for dinks, third shot drops, third shot drives and no man’s land defense.
