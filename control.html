Playing in Control - The Five C's
The key concept to take away from the Moving Beyond 3.5 clinics is that of playing in control. A number of mental approaches to help you do this include:
  • Calm - being calm is about mental strength, play in the present, smile and remember you are playing a game, do not think about winning or losing and replace that voice in your head with an awareness of your body, where your paddle is pointing, your opponents location etc.
  • Concentration - narrow, external focus
  • Consistent - play shots that are within your capabilities by hitting fewer types of shots well when drilling and playing games
  • Calibrated - use the warmup to dial in how hard to hit the ball and how close to lines is safe etc. to improve your consistency
  • Confidence - as your consistency improves so will your confidence to the point you are genuinely surprised if you miss
